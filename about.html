Research Insights

Three Things We Learned From Industry

1

Surplus Is Normal — Even for Responsible Factories

Companies try to reduce waste through planning and automation, but surplus still happens due to demand volatility, order cancellations, MOQ constraints, and production overage. This isn't failure — it's the nature of manufacturing.

2

Trust Is the Biggest Barrier to Redistribution

Factories care about where surplus goes — not just financially, but reputationally. There's hesitation because of unclear redistribution partners, fear of brand leakage, and uncertainty about how surplus will be handled. This is the problem we design around.

3

Existing Relationships Work — But Don't Scale

Resellers told us direct outreach can be good business because it builds rapport. But the process is manual, relationship-dependent, and doesn't scale efficiently across regions. CircuLink is the infrastructure that makes those relationships scalable.

"The core problem is: surplus exists, but redistribution is fragmented, manual, and trust-limited. That's what CircuLink is built to fix."

Our Goals

Three Outcomes We're Building Toward

CircuLink isn't just a marketplace. It's infrastructure for a more circular textile economy in Southeast Asia. We're targeting outcomes across three dimensions.

Join the Pilot Cohort
ENV
Environmental

Reduce virgin fabric demand by circulating deadstock. We measure success in kg and tonnes of fabric circulated, CO₂ avoided, and water saved versus new production.

SYS
Systemic

Activate real transactions and shorten time-to-redistribution so material value doesn't degrade. Build transaction histories that reduce uncertainty and increase repeat deals.

SOC
Social

Empower ASEAN SMEs and independent designers with access and visibility — improving their competitiveness globally by giving them access to quality materials they couldn't previously reach.

Go-To-Market

Our Three-Phase Roadmap

We execute in phases — validating assumptions before scaling, and scaling where we have authentic access.

Phase 1 · Weeks 1–4

Validate

Confirm surplus types, buying criteria and trust requirements with a small cohort of suppliers and buyers. 5 suppliers, 10 buyers, 90-day pilot across India, Indonesia and Singapore.

Phase 2 · Weeks 5–10

Prototype & Onboard

Use our MVP to onboard initial suppliers and verified buyers. Run early transactions, refine the workflow, and gather data on what standardised listing attributes matter most to buyers.

Phase 3 · Month 3+

Scale Regionally

Expand across India, Indonesia, Myanmar and Singapore. We chose these four because we have touchpoints and cultural access. Long-term vision: become the trusted marketplace standard for textile surplus across all of Southeast Asia.
